### ğŸ“ˆ **Project Tracking Indicators**

These indicators help measure the different aspects of project performance:

>[!tip] The right mix of indicators can give a comprehensive view of a project's health.

- **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**: Metrics that quantify project success and progress.
    
- **Milestones**: Key events or achievements that signal progress toward project completion.
    
- **Budget and Cost Metrics**: Monitoring expenditures and financial health against the budget.
    
- **Schedule Metrics**: Assessing timelines, deadlines, and progress toward project completion.
    
- **Scope Metrics**: Evaluating the project's scope and any changes made during the project lifecycle.
    

### ğŸ” **Steps to Evaluate Indicators and Analyze Variances**

1. **Define Baseline Metrics**: Establish baseline metrics at the start of the project to compare against during evaluation.
    
2. **Regularly Collect Data**: Continuously gather data related to tracking indicators throughout the project.
    
3. **Compare Actual vs. Baseline**: Identify variances by comparing actual data with baseline metrics.
    
4. **Identify the Causes**: Investigate root causes of variances (e.g., scope changes, resource issues).
    
5. **Analyze Impact**: Assess how variances affect timelines, costs, and project scope.
    
6. **Take Corrective Actions**: Develop and implement strategies to address negative variances and bring the project back on track.
    
7. **Communicate Findings**: Share progress, findings, and corrective actions with the team and stakeholders.
